# Hypothesis Testing and Statistical Analysis using SAS

## Objective

This project aims to apply statistical hypothesis testing techniques to analyze real-world data using SAS. 
The goal is to compare group performances and investigate relationships between variables using appropriate statistical methods.

---

## Dataset

The project is based on two datalines:

1. **Classroom Performance Dataset**

   * Compares student performance under two teaching approaches:

     * Cooperative learning
     * Competitive learning

2. **Birth Weight Dataset**

   * Contains information about infant birth weight and related factors such as:

     * Mother's age
     * Smoking habits
     * Education level
     * Prenatal visits
     * Weight gain during pregnancy

---

## Tools

* SAS 

---

## Methodology

### Part 1: Descriptive Statistical Analysis

* Computed measures of central tendency:

  * Mean
  * Median
* Computed measures of variation:

  * Standard deviation
  * Range
* Analyzed distribution shape:

  * Skewness
  * Kurtosis

### Part 2: Hypothesis Testing (T-test)

* Conducted T-tests to compare:

  * Cooperative vs Competitive teaching methods
  * Birth weight across different categorical variables (e.g., smoking vs non-smoking mothers)

* Verified assumptions:

  * Normality of data
  * Independence of observations
  * Homogeneity of variance

---

## Key Findings

### Classroom Analysis

* Differences were observed between cooperative and competitive learning approaches.
* Measures of central tendency and variability highlighted performance differences across groups.

### Birth Weight Analysis

* Infant birth weight showed variation based on maternal characteristics.
* Smoking and other factors had measurable impacts on birth weight.

---

## Business Insight (Non-Technical)

* Teaching methods can influence student performance outcomes.
* Maternal lifestyle and health factors significantly affect infant birth weight.
* These insights can help in improving educational strategies and healthcare awareness.
